# ED-02 / R1-18 — added value and research directions (ticket 0278)

Response-letter bullets for ticket 0283. Revision: expanded Concluding
Remarks (`data-paper.qmd`, section 4).

- **ED-02 (added value, research directions).** The conclusion now discusses
  the research directions the database enables, structured by method family:
  citation-graph analysis, semantic methods on the deposited embeddings,
  lexical tracking, and descriptive statistics. It names the further
  directions the editor suggested — institution and co-author networks to map
  the field's key actors, text analysis and content classification of the
  literature's shifting focus — and briefly references the research questions
  of the Œconomia companion paper (crystallisation of climate finance as a
  category of economic governance, three-act periodization), including the
  epistemic role the companion assigns to the corpus: corroboration of a
  documentary history, not discovery or falsification.

- **R1-18 (HET potential hard to assess; cleaning needed).** Answered
  candidly in the same section: any focused research question will demand
  task-specific cleaning beyond the generic filtering documented in the
  paper; the corpus is offered as reusable infrastructure, not a finished
  analysis. The conclusion illustrates both the limit and what survives it
  from our own use: community-separation tests fail on the thin pre-2007
  window, yet qualitative results stand on simple counts (Negishi absent
  from the pre-2007 canon; Barrett co-cited with the carbon-pricing
  literature). The companion paper under review at Œconomia is cited as the
  proof of use.
