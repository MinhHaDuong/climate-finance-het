**R1-06 / R1-08 (why no national-institution grey literature?).** The referee
is right that the corpus excludes documents from national institutions
(government and parliamentary reports, economic analysis councils, central
bank communications). We now state and justify this boundary explicitly in
Section 2.3. Two reasons. First, the boundary follows the research object:
the corpus traces climate finance as a category of *international* economic
governance, defined, negotiated, and accounted for in the UNFCCC and OECD
arenas, whereas national documents largely apply that category domestically
rather than constitute it. Second, tractability: national-institution
documents have no common index or point of access, and each document class is
a corpus-scale universe of its own — the central-bank-communication dataset
of Campiglio, Deyris, Romelli and Scalisi (2025), which the referee kindly
points us to, holds 35,487 speeches from 131 central banks for that single
class. Sampling a few national sources would add noise without coverage;
harvesting them systematically is a different project. We did consider the
incorporation the referee asks about, and we acknowledge its appeal for
multilingual coverage: the revised text names it as the natural extension
path, notes that the provenance architecture accepts such a layer without
reprocessing existing sources, and cites Campiglio et al. (2025) as the
first curated open dataset to plug in. Complementarily, *within* the
international-institution frame we did extend coverage in this revision: a
curated layer of UNFCCC and OECD DAC key documents (COP decisions on finance,
Biennial Assessments, Rio-marker reporting directives) joins the corpus —
see the response to this remark under the corpus-v2 changes.
