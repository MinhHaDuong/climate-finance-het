Response-letter bullet (feeds ticket 0283; letter-voice: first person

> **R1-19** (CSV organization). I kept the file layout. The file is a
> standard rectangular CSV (26 columns, no duplicate rows) that loads
> directly into pandas or R, and its columns already follow four logical
> groups in a fixed order: record identity (`source`, `source_id`, `doi`),
> bibliographic metadata (title through affiliations), provenance flags
> (the `from_*` columns and provenance fields), and curation metadata (the
> quality flags and protection fields). Reordering or reshaping the columns
> would change a published Zenodo artifact and break every downstream
> consumer of the deposit for a viewer-ergonomics gain. Instead, the
> revision makes the structure explicit for manual exploration: Table
> @tbl-variables now shows each column's logical group, and the deposit
> ships a formal data dictionary (`codebook.md`) giving, per column, the
> type, the allowed values, and the share of missing values measured on the
> deposited file. The codebook is generated by the pipeline from the same
> declared column contract that the export script enforces when writing the
> CSV, so it cannot drift from the data.
