## Pre-2007 separation of the three traditions {#sec:pre2007-separation}

The article (appendix A.5) reports the three pre-2007 intellectual traditions --- environmental economics, development economics, international effort-sharing --- as distinct, and rests that separation on the intellectual-historical record rather than on the corpus. Three quantitative tests of the separation were attempted on the pre-2007 co-citation slice. On that slice none produces a statistic distinguishable from its null.

- **Co-citation community separation.** A Louvain partition of the 250 most-cited pre-2007 references (84 nodes, 369 edges) divides them strongly: within-tradition co-citation share $0.89$ against a degree-preserving configuration-model null of $0.37$ ($z \approx 25$, $N = 1000$ permutations). That partition is optimised on the same graph the null tests, so the separation is circular. Fixing tradition membership a priori from the founding works of the three traditions (the anchors of the main-article traditions table) removes the circularity but makes the test degenerate: only about three co-citation-core nodes match, all environmental economics (development and effort-sharing match none), because the aid and burden-sharing founding works fall below the co-citation core of this slice. No independent separation result survives.

- **Sparsity-conditioned null.** With the labelling kept independent of the graph, the within-tradition cohesion is not distinguishable from a degree-preserving configuration-model null. The apparent separation of the fitted partition is not in excess of what the degree sequence alone produces at this density.

- **Temporal cross-tradition coupling.** Cross-tradition citation shows no decline surviving a per-year permutation null through crystallization: the observed trend is not statistically different from the null, and the density-controlled trend, if anything, runs the other way (cross-tradition citation rising, not falling).

The pre-2007 slice is the sparsest in the corpus (retro-indexing skew; grey literature under-referenced), and the traditions' founding works barely enter it as cited references. It is too sparse for these tests to be conclusive. The permutation null ($N = 1000$; seed and window bounds in `config/analysis.yaml`) and the temporal-coupling scout outputs are archived in the reproducibility materials.
